Build the Customer Hub module for CompanyHQ. This is a customer-facing portal where Chapin Landscapes customers can view their jobs, access documents, read care guides, and message the team. Read everything below before writing any code.
OVERVIEW
The Customer Hub is a separate authenticated portal inside the same app. Customers log in with their own credentials and see ONLY their own data. They have no access to any internal CompanyHQ modules (no HR, no SOPs, no equipment, etc.). The hub has five sections: Dashboard, My Jobs, Documents, Care Library, and Messages.
SECTION 1: AUTHENTICATION & ROUTING
Add a 'customer' role to the existing role system. When a user with role='customer' logs in, route them to /customer-hub instead of the main app dashboard. Customer accounts are created two ways: (1) automatically when a job is created and the customer field has an email, (2) manually via 'Invite Customer' button on a job record. Account creation sends a welcome email with a password-setup link. Customers cannot register themselves — all accounts are created by Chapin staff.
SECTION 2: CUSTOMER DASHBOARD
The dashboard shows: (1) Personalized welcome with their name. (2) Active job card — if they have a job with status not 'Complete', show it prominently with job name, current status, and scheduled date. (3) Unread messages count with link to Messages. (4) Action items — any documents requiring their signature or review. (5) Quick links to Documents, Care Library, Service History. (6) A seasonal tip card — rotate monthly based on current month.
SECTION 3: MY JOBS
Show all jobs linked to the customer's account. Each job card shows: job name, status, scheduled dates, crew lead name. Clicking opens a full job detail page with: scope of work, materials used, before/after photos, crew notes (flagged as customer-visible by manager), linked documents for that job, and care guide links. Also show a full Service History tab — chronological list of every job and visit.
SECTION 4: DOCUMENTS
Show all documents linked to the customer's account, organized into folders: Proposals, Contracts, Invoices, Warranties, Care Guides, Photos, Other. Each document shows name, date, status. Customers can view in browser and download as PDF. Add a 'Request a Document' button that sends a message to the Chapin office. When a new document is uploaded to a customer's account in the admin side, they receive an in-app notification and email.
SECTION 5: CARE LIBRARY
A searchable library of care guides organized into 8 categories: Hardscape & Patios, Plants & Landscape Beds, Lawn Care, Irrigation Systems, Outdoor Living Features, Seasonal Guides, Troubleshooting, Snow & Winter. Each guide is a rich-text article with a title, category, content body, and optional PDF download. Guides are created and managed by Chapin admins/managers in the CompanyHQ admin side. Customers see all guides but 'My Guides' tab highlights guides tagged to their specific services. Add full-text search. Add a seasonal filter. Each guide is saveable/bookmarkable per customer.
SECTION 6: MESSAGES
Two-way messaging between customers and the Chapin team. Customer sees their conversation threads. New message composer has a topic dropdown: Question about my job / Document request / Scheduling / Billing question / Maintenance request / Other. The topic routes the message to the right team member in CompanyHQ's internal Messages system. Customers receive email notifications when they get a reply. All messages logged permanently. On the Chapin side, customer messages appear in the main Messages module with a 'Customer' label.
SECTION 7: AUTOMATED NOTIFICATIONS
Build triggers for these automated messages (email + in-app): (1) Job scheduled → confirmation message with date and crew lead. (2) Job status changes to In Progress → 'Your crew has started work today.' (3) Job Complete → 'Your job is complete — view photos and documents in your account.' (4) New document uploaded → 'A new document is available: [name].' (5) New message reply from Chapin → 'You have a new message from the Chapin team.' (6) Invoice ready → 'Your invoice is ready to view.' Use the existing email infrastructure. Notification content should be editable by admins.
SECTION 8: DATABASE SCHEMA
Add/update these tables using Drizzle: customers (linked to users table with role=customer, plus property address, preferred contact method), customer_jobs (links customers to job records), customer_documents (documents visible to a specific customer with folder category), care_guides (title, category, content, pdf_url, tags, is_published, created_by, created_at), customer_saved_guides (which guides a customer has bookmarked), customer_job_services (tags on a job that link to relevant care guide categories), customer_notifications (in-app notification log per customer).
SECTION 9: ADMIN SIDE (CHAPIN STAFF VIEW)
In the existing CompanyHQ admin/manager view, add: (1) On each Job record — a 'Customer Hub' tab showing what the customer sees for that job, an 'Invite Customer' button, a 'Customer Visible Notes' field separate from internal crew notes, and an 'Upload Document for Customer' button. (2) In a new Customer Accounts section — list of all customer accounts, ability to create/invite, view their hub, reset their password, and deactivate. (3) Care Guide Manager — create, edit, delete, and publish care guides. Assign categories and tags. (4) Customer messages appear in the main Messages module with a Customer badge.
SECTION 10: UI REQUIREMENTS
The Customer Hub must feel like a polished consumer app — not an internal tool. Use the Chapin Landscapes brand colors (dark green #1E3A2F, gold #C9A84C, cream #F7F3EC). Navigation is a clean bottom tab bar on mobile, left sidebar on desktop. Every page works perfectly on iPhone Safari and Android Chrome. No technical jargon in any customer-facing text. All loading states handled gracefully. Friendly empty states when there is no data ('No jobs yet — your project details will appear here once your job is scheduled.'). Error messages in plain English.
BUILD ORDER
1. Database schema updates and migrations. 2. Authentication routing for customer role. 3. Customer dashboard. 4. My Jobs and Service History. 5. Documents portal. 6. Care Library with admin content manager. 7. Messages. 8. Automated notification triggers. 9. Admin-side customer management tools. 10. First-login welcome tour. Do not use mock data — all data must persist to the real database.
