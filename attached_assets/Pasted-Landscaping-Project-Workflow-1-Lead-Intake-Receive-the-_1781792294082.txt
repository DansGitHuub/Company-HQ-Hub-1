Landscaping Project Workflow
1. Lead Intake
Receive the initial customer contact by phone, web form, referral, text, or email.

Capture the customer name, contact information, address, and basic job type.

Enter the lead into the system immediately.

2. Lead Qualification
Review the job type, scope, location, and expected fit.

Decide whether the project is a fit for your company.

Decide whether a site visit is needed or whether photos and remote information are enough.

If the project is not a fit, decline or refer it appropriately.

3. Site Visit or Remote Review
For most jobs, schedule a site visit.

For smaller or simpler jobs, review photos and information remotely when appropriate.

During the site visit, take measurements and photos.

Capture wide shots, close-ups, and any problem areas.

Record access issues, site conditions, and anything that may affect the job.

4. Estimate and Proposal
Build the estimate from measurements, photos, and site notes.

Prepare a clear proposal that defines scope, assumptions, and pricing.

Send the proposal electronically unless an in-person review makes more sense for a larger job.

For complex projects, plan for follow-up meetings to review materials, heights, samples, plants, layout, and other decisions.

5. Follow-Up and Approval
Follow up on every proposal.

Answer customer questions and revise as needed.

Track whether the proposal is open, pending, revised, or approved.

When the customer accepts, collect the deposit, generally 50%.

Mark the job as sold only after payment is received.

6. Production Handoff
Assign a crew and schedule the work on the calendar.

Create a job packet that includes scope, drawings, schematics, photos, measurements, notes, and special instructions.

Confirm whether materials are stocked or must be ordered.

Make sure all required materials are secured before the start date whenever possible.

Review the job with the crew lead ahead of time, preferably on site for larger jobs.

7. Job Readiness
Confirm the crew has access to all job information before the start date.

Confirm the timeline, sequence of work, and any customer promises.

Confirm equipment, materials, and documents are ready.

Confirm who to call if something is unclear.

Do not let the crew guess when a written answer should exist.

8. Job Start
Crew clocks in through the app.

Crew reviews the job details before starting.

Crew confirms the first day’s plan with the crew lead.

Crew begins work only after the job packet and materials are ready.

9. Job Execution
Crew tracks time and materials in the app.

Crew follows the written instructions and approved plan.

Crew takes progress photos on schedule.

Crew records notable issues, delays, or field changes immediately.

Questions should be answered from the job packet whenever possible.

10. Milestone Checkpoints
For larger jobs, break the work into checkpoints.

Examples include patio complete, deck complete, grading complete, planting complete, or irrigation complete.

At each checkpoint, take photos and verify the work before moving forward.

Get customer sign-off when appropriate.

Do not continue hidden work without documenting the completed phase.

11. Change Orders
If the customer changes the scope, materials, design, or expectations, stop and document it.

Record what changed, why it changed, and what it will cost.

Get approval before proceeding with the changed work.

Update the job packet so the crew is working from the current plan.

12. Quality Check
Complete an internal review at the end of the job.

Confirm that the work matches the approved scope.

Confirm punch-list items are complete.

Verify the site is clean and ready for customer review.

13. Customer Walkthrough
Walk the completed job with the customer.

Review what was completed and confirm satisfaction.

Address any final punch-list items.

Obtain final approval or sign-off when appropriate.

14. Final Invoice and Closeout
Send the final invoice after approval.

Collect the remaining balance.

Save final photos, notes, and closeout documents.

Store any warranty, maintenance, or care instructions.

Record lessons learned for future jobs.

Standards to Enforce
Every job should have one source of truth.

Every job should have a complete packet before the crew starts.

Every crew should know the next step before starting the current one.

Every larger job should have checkpoints.

Every scope change should become a documented change order.

Every completed job should have a closeout record.
